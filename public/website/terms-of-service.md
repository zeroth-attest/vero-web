## 3. How Vero Works

### 3.1 Trust Anchor Authentication

When you use Vero to verify your identity, you will be asked to log in through a supported third-party authentication provider (the Trust Anchor). This login is performed solely through the Trust Anchor's own authentication flow (such as OAuth). Vero uses this login exclusively for the purpose of confirming that you control the account associated with that Trust Anchor at that moment in time.

**Important:** Vero does not store your Trust Anchor login credentials. Vero does not retain session tokens, access tokens, or refresh tokens beyond the immediate verification session. Vero cannot and does not use your Trust Anchor login to take any actions on your behalf, post content, read your messages, access your contacts, or interact with your account in any way beyond confirming your identity.

### 3.2 Information Shared During Verification

When you authenticate through a Trust Anchor, certain personally identifiable information ("PII") associated with your Trust Anchor account -- such as your display name, email address, profile photo, or username -- may be disclosed to the verifying party as part of the verification result. This is the core function of the App: enabling the verifying party to confirm that you are who you claim to be by seeing the identity information associated with your authenticated Trust Anchor account.

**You acknowledge and agree that by completing a verification session, the PII returned by the Trust Anchor will be shared in real time with the requesting (verifying) party.** This information is transmitted directly and is not stored on any Vero server.

### 3.3 No Server-Side Storage of PII

Vero is designed with a privacy-first architecture. We do not store any user PII on our servers. Verification data is transmitted in real time between the presenting party and the verifying party and is not persisted in any Vero-operated database, server, or cloud storage after the session concludes. We do not maintain user profiles, identity records, or authentication histories on our infrastructure.

### 3.4 Trusted Contacts (Local Storage Feature)

Vero may offer a feature that allows you to save verified contacts locally on your device ("Trusted Contacts"). This data is stored exclusively on your device and is never uploaded to or synchronized with any Vero server. You are solely responsible for the security of your device and the locally stored Trusted Contacts data.
