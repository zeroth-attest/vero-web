# Vero -- Privacy Policy

**Last Updated:** April 9, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Zeroth Attest, Inc. ("Zeroth Attest," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and shares information in connection with the Vero identity verification application (the "App") and the website at vero.technology (the "Site"). By using the App or Site, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy.

## 1. Overview

Vero is an identity verification tool that allows one person to confirm another person's identity by having them authenticate through a third-party service of their choice (a "Trust Anchor"). Vero is built on a privacy-first principle: we do not store personally identifiable information ("PII") on our servers. This Privacy Policy explains what information flows through the App, what we do and do not retain, and your rights regarding that information.

## 2. Information We Do NOT Collect or Store

Understanding what we do not do is central to understanding Vero's privacy model.

**We do not store PII on our servers.** When you authenticate through a Trust Anchor during a verification session, profile information such as your name, email address, profile photo, or username may be transmitted in real time to the verifying party. This data passes through the verification session and is not persisted in any Vero-operated database, server, or cloud storage.

**We do not store Trust Anchor credentials.** Vero never receives, stores, or has access to your passwords for any third-party service. Authentication is handled entirely by the Trust Anchor's own login flow (such as OAuth). We do not retain access tokens, refresh tokens, or session tokens beyond the immediate verification session.

**We do not create user accounts or profiles.** Vero does not maintain user accounts, identity records, or authentication histories on our infrastructure.

**We cannot take actions on your behalf.** Vero requests only the minimum permissions necessary to retrieve basic profile information from a Trust Anchor. We cannot and do not post content, read messages, access contacts, or interact with your third-party accounts in any way beyond the one-time identity confirmation.

## 3. Information That Is Shared During a Verification Session

When you complete a verification session, the Trust Anchor you authenticate with may return certain profile information -- typically your display name, email address, profile photo, and/or username. This information is shared in real time with the verifying party (the person who requested the verification). This is the core function of the App and occurs with your knowledge and consent each time you initiate a verification.

The verifying party receives this information directly. Vero does not control how the verifying party handles the information after it is disclosed. We encourage all users to handle PII responsibly and in compliance with applicable data protection laws. Our Terms of Service require verifying parties to use disclosed PII solely for the purpose of identity confirmation and not for unrelated purposes such as marketing, resale, or unsolicited contact.

## 4. Information We May Collect

While we do not store PII, we may collect limited technical and usage data to operate, maintain, and improve the App.

### 4.1 Technical and Usage Data

We may collect non-identifying technical information such as:

- Device type, operating system, and version
- App version
- General usage patterns (for example, which Trust Anchors are most commonly selected, session success or failure rates)
- Crash reports and error logs
- IP addresses (used transiently for routing and security purposes, not stored long-term in association with any user identity)

This data is used for service operation, troubleshooting, security monitoring, and product improvement. It is not linked to any individual's PII because we do not maintain user profiles or accounts.

### 4.2 Cookies and Similar Technologies

The Site (vero.technology) may use cookies or similar technologies for basic site functionality and analytics. These do not track individual users across the web and do not collect PII. You may adjust your browser settings to refuse cookies, though some site features may not function properly.

## 5. Trusted Contacts (Local Storage)

Vero may offer a feature allowing you to store verified contacts locally on your device ("Trusted Contacts"). This information is stored exclusively on your device's local storage. It is never uploaded to, synchronized with, or backed up to any Vero server.

Because this data resides only on your device, you are responsible for its security. If your device is lost, stolen, damaged, or if you uninstall the App, this data may be permanently lost. Vero cannot recover locally stored data.

## 6. Third-Party Authentication Providers

When you authenticate through a Trust Anchor, you are interacting with that provider's systems. Each Trust Anchor has its own privacy policy and data practices that govern how it handles your information. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any Trust Anchor you use.

Vero does not control what information a Trust Anchor makes available during authentication, nor do we control how a Trust Anchor processes or retains data about your login activity on its platform. For example, a Trust Anchor may log the fact that you used its service to authenticate with a third-party application.

## 7. How We Use Information

The limited technical data we collect (described in Section 4) is used for the following purposes:

- **Service operation:** Ensuring the App functions correctly and verification sessions complete successfully.
- **Security:** Detecting and preventing abuse, fraud, or unauthorized access.
- **Improvement:** Understanding usage patterns to improve App reliability and the user experience.
- **Legal compliance:** Meeting applicable legal obligations or responding to lawful requests from authorities.

We do not sell, rent, or trade any user information to third parties for marketing or advertising purposes. We do not use your data for targeted advertising.

## 8. Data Sharing

Because we do not store PII, our data-sharing footprint is minimal. We may share limited technical data with:

- **Service providers:** Third parties that assist us with hosting, analytics, or error monitoring, who are contractually obligated to protect the data and use it only for the services they provide to us.
- **Legal requirements:** If required by law, regulation, subpoena, court order, or other governmental request, we may disclose information we possess to the extent necessary to comply.
- **Business transfers:** In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, any data we hold may be transferred as part of the transaction. We will notify you of any such change in ownership or control.

## 9. Data Security

We implement commercially reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect the limited data we do process. Because we do not store PII on our servers, the primary security risk related to personal information exists during the real-time transmission of verification data between parties. We use encryption in transit (TLS/SSL) to protect data during transmission.

No system is completely secure. While we strive to protect the data that passes through our systems, we cannot guarantee absolute security, and you use the App at your own risk.

## 10. Children's Privacy

The App is not intended for use by anyone under the age of 18 (or the age of majority in their jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect information from minors. If we become aware that a minor has used the App, we will take steps to address the situation, though given that we do not store PII, there is typically no user data for us to delete.

## 11. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal data, such as the right to access, correct, delete, or port your data, or to object to or restrict certain processing.

Because Vero does not store PII on its servers, most of these rights are satisfied by design -- there is no personal data in our systems to access, correct, or delete. For Trusted Contacts stored locally on your device, you have full control: you can view, modify, or delete that data directly within the App or by removing the App from your device.

If you have questions or concerns about your data rights, you may contact us at support@vero.technology.

### 11.1 California Residents

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) grants you certain additional rights. Because we do not sell personal information and do not store PII, our practices are substantially aligned with CCPA requirements by design. You may still contact us to exercise any applicable rights or to ask questions.

### 11.2 European Economic Area, United Kingdom, and Switzerland Residents

If you are located in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, you may have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or equivalent local law. Our legal basis for any limited processing of technical data is our legitimate interest in operating and securing the App. Because we do not store PII, the GDPR's data subject rights (access, erasure, portability, etc.) are largely satisfied by our architecture. You may contact us or lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority if you have concerns.

Your continued use of the App after any change constitutes your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.
